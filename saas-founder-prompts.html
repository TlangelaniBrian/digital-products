1. Customer Discovery & Problem Validation

Identify your ICP's top 5 frustrations

Act as a SaaS customer researcher. I'm building a [product type] for [target audience]. Based on your knowledge of this market, what are the top 5 frustrations these customers face daily? For each frustration, provide: (1) the exact pain point, (2) how they currently solve it, (3) why the current solution is inadequate, and (4) a suggested positioning angle for my product. Be specific and evidence-based.

Conduct a Jobs-to-be-Done interview protocol

Create a structured JTBD interview protocol for [target customer role] making a decision about [problem area]. Include: (1) trigger questions to identify switching moments, (2) questions about their current workarounds, (3) questions to uncover the emotional and social dimensions of the job, (4) questions to understand who else is involved in the decision. Make it practical enough to use in real customer calls.

Map the customer's current workflow without your product

For a customer who needs [outcome/result] but doesn't yet use my [product type], describe their likely current workflow step-by-step. For each step, note: (1) the tool or method they use, (2) the time it takes, (3) the frustration level (1-10), (4) where data gets lost or duplicated. Then suggest where my product could replace or eliminate steps.

3. Cold Outreach & Lead Generation

Write a cold email sequence that gets replies

Write a 5-email cold outreach sequence for [my SaaS product] targeting [ideal customer persona] at [company type/size]. For each email: (1) subject line (with 2 variants), (2) opening hook that references a specific trigger event or pain point, (3) value proposition in one sentence, (4) soft CTA, (5) the psychological principle behind the approach. Make the tone direct, non-salesy, and respect their time. Follow best practices from 30-50%+ reply-rate sequences.

Generate 20 LinkedIn DM openers by persona

I sell [product]. Generate 20 LinkedIn DM openers — 5 each for: (1) VP of Engineering, (2) CTO, (3) Head of Product, (4) Founder/CEO. For each opener: keep it under 300 characters, avoid pitch language, reference a trigger event or observation specific to their role, and end with a low-friction question. Prioritise openers that have worked in B2B SaaS according to what you've seen.

Make the tone collaborative, not transactional. The partnership is for [my product description].
